# Product Brief: Kode With Greg

## Executive Summary

Kode With Greg is a local, in-person teaching service run by Greg out of his garage. It exists to demystify AI-assisted web building for everyday people — kids, adults, and curious tinkerers — by teaching them to use LLMs, generative AI, and the BMad Method to build real websites they're proud of. No Zoom calls, no passive video courses: just hands-on sessions in a low-pressure, community environment where people make things.

The website is the front door. Its job is simple: make someone who hears about Kode With Greg feel like they belong there, understand what they'll walk away with, and know how to show up.

## The Problem

Most people who want a website either pay someone else or give up. The "build it yourself" path is full of friction — confusing tools, jargon-heavy tutorials, and AI workflows that assume you already know what you're doing. The hobbyist who wants a personal blog, a small business page, or a portfolio has no guided, hands-on path that meets them where they are.

YouTube tutorials exist, but they're passive. Online courses exist, but they require discipline. What's missing is someone in the room with you, helping you move from "I have no idea" to "I built this."

## The Solution

Greg teaches small groups in person — in his garage — how to use modern AI tools to go from idea to working project. Sessions are goal-grouped: when someone reaches out, Greg assesses what they want to build or learn, then groups them with others who share the same goal. Each Saturday session is built around that group's specific focus. You come in with an idea; you leave having made real progress alongside people working toward the same thing.

The curriculum spans the full modern stack: understanding the LLM landscape (which tools exist, how to evaluate and pay for them, which suite fits your goals), setting up VSCode, working with LLMs (Claude, Codex, Gemini), using ComfyUI for generative AI, deploying to AWS or Azure, and understanding the cloud services those platforms provide. Greg also teaches the BMad Method — a structured, AI-assisted workflow for planning and building software.

The Kode With Greg website markets this service: who it's for, what you'll learn, and how to get a spot.

## Who This Serves

**Primary: The Curious Hobbyist**
Adults and kids who want to build something — a blog, a portfolio, a small business site — but don't know where to start. They've likely heard of AI and ChatGPT but haven't figured out how to make it work for them. They want results, not theory, and respond to approachable, friendly instruction over technical depth.

**Secondary: Local Parents**
Parents looking for a practical, modern tech activity for their kids that isn't gaming or passive screen time.

## What Makes This Different

- **In-person, hands-on.** Not a video. Not a chatbot. A real person next to you when you get stuck.
- **Accessible to everyone.** Kids and adults. Total beginners welcome. No prior coding or AI experience required.
- **Garage energy.** Low-pressure, informal, community feel — the opposite of a corporate training course.
- **BMad + LLM-native curriculum.** Teaches current, practical AI workflows (BMad Method, LLM prompting, generative AI tools) rather than the legacy "learn to code" curricula that AI is already disrupting.
- **Real practitioner, not a YouTuber.** Greg is actively building with these tools professionally — sharing live workflow knowledge from the front lines, not explaining theory.

## About Greg

Greg Pascucci has been a professional software developer since 2005, with deep expertise in Java, enterprise databases (PostgreSQL, Oracle), and Linux systems. For the past three years he has worked as a full-stack developer across React, Vue, Angular, Node, NestJS, Express, and Spring Boot, on Scrum/Agile teams for over 15 years.

Since 2024 he has been all-in on LLMs — not just as a user. As lead developer at his company, Greg is responsible for inventing new processes and patterns using AI tools including BMad, Claude Code, Codex CLI, Gemini CLI, and GitHub Copilot. He builds and tinkers across Mac, Windows, Linux, and a pair of Raspberry Pis running custom setups at home.

His belief is simple: LLMs have changed the game so completely that anyone — regardless of background — can now bring their ideas to life with the right guidance. Kode With Greg exists to fill that gap: to take the workflows Greg uses professionally and translate them into something a hobbyist or first-time entrepreneur can act on in a single Saturday session.

## Success Criteria

- The website communicates what this is in under 10 seconds
- People in Greg's local community can find the site and know how to book or reach out
- 3 paying attendees every Saturday ($600/week) within the first few months
- Greg feels proud to hand someone the URL

## Scope (MVP Website)

**In:**
- Homepage — who this is for, what you'll learn, Greg's story and credibility
- Services / Sessions page — what a session looks like, topics covered
- Clear call-to-action throughout: every page funnels toward contacting Greg to book a session
- Contact page — email and cell phone number to reach Greg and set up a session
- Mobile-friendly, professional design

**Out (for now):**
- Student showcase / project gallery
- Blog or resource library
- Paid online courses or video content
- Automated booking / calendar system

## Pricing

$200 per person per session. Payment accepted via cash or e-transfer. Sessions run Saturdays.

## Vision

Kode With Greg becomes the go-to local resource for anyone who wants to build with AI tools — a small community hub where beginners become builders. If it grows, Greg could expand to weekend workshops, bring in guest instructors, or offer a structured curriculum. The garage is just the start.
